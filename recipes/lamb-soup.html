Pickled Cabbage & Lamp Soup (酸菜羊肉汤)

A Comforting Winter Soup

This is a classic winter soup hailing from the Northeast provinces of China where it is often bitterly cold. Famous for their cauldrons of stew, this dish is certainly a must when visiting the countryside. They even have a major tourist attraction in Harbin called "Ice and Snow World" where I visited a few years ago and had all my devices promptly die because it was just that cold! Composed of saurkraut, rice noodles, and thinly sliced lamb meat, its a soup sure to warm your soul. Much like a shabu-shabu or hotpot, most of the cooking happens after the dish is taken off the stove. Though I didn't get to enjoy this dish as much as I would have liked growing up due to the cost of lamb, it was still one of the dishes I always asked for when it came to winter and my birthday (which is in winter)! Though I'll admit I am a bit of a soup fiend in general.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1-2 lbs thinly sliced Lamb
  • 1 pack Pickled Cabbage
  • 2 bundles Bean Thread Noodles
  • 1 pinch White Pepper
  • 2-3 cloves Garlics
  • 1 Ginger
  • 2 Green Onions
  • 1 bunch Cilantro
  • 1 pinch Dried Goji
  • Optional: Dried Shrimp, Chicken Broth & Peppercorns

Steps

  1. Prepare lamb by blanching with peppercorns then rinsing in cold water.
  2. Prepare rice noodles by soaking in boiled water for 5-10 min and straining.
  3. Saute diced garlic, ginger, and green onions in heated oil with white pepper.
  4. Prepare thinly sliced pickled cabbage, rinse and squeeze out water before adding to saute and mixing for 1-2 mins.
  5. Add water/chicken broth (alternatively boil dried shrimp in water) and bring back to a boil.
  6. Add in lamb and noodles and remove from stove.
  7. Garnish with cilantro and dried goji then serve to be shared at the center of a table!
