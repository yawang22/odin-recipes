Beef Chow Fun (乾炒牛河)

A Classic Chinese Dish

Chow Fun, also known as Chow Hoh Fun, is a a staple Cantonese dish made from stir-frying beef, wide rice noodles, and bean sprouts. It gets its name from the quaint town of Shahe in Guangzhou, where its distinctive style of flat rice noodles was first created. As more Chinese people started coming to America in the 40s and 50s, over two-thirds of them were Guangdong Yan. And up until the 60s and 70s, Chinese food in America was primarily Cantonese cuisine. That's why, today, Cantonese dishes like Chow Fun and Chow Mein are still among the most popular dishes across over 40,000 Chinese restaurants in America.

While there are many variations of this dish made today in America, this is by far the most common version found in China. Growing up, I often made this as one of my go to dish of comfort as it was simple and always reminded me of home no matter where I was. I hope by making this, you too can enjoy a quick and simple taste of China!

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 10 oz Flank Steak
  • 1 lb Fresh Flat Rice Noodles
  • 3 stems Green Onions
  • 0.5 Yellow Onions
  • 1 handful Bean Sprouts
  • 4 tbsp Cooking Oil
  • 1 tsp Sesame Oil

Beef Marinade

  • .25 tsp White Pepper
  • 1 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1 tsp Tamari Sauce
  • 1 tsp Cooking Wine
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1 tbsp Cornstarch
  • 1 tbsp Oyster Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 2 tbsp Water

Chow Fun Sauce

  • 1 tsp Tamari Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1 tbsp Oyster Sauce

Steps

  1. Cut beef into thin 1/4 inch slices and let soak in room temperature water.
  2. Wash all vegatables. Peel and thinly slice the yellow onion to the same thickness as the beef and chop the green onions into 1 inch long pieces, separating the white parts from the green parts.
  3. Create Beef Marinade by mixing all marinade ingredients together until smooth.
  4. Squeeze all excess water from beef before mixing with the marinade. Add 1 tbsp of cooking oil to the beef to seal the juices.
  5. Prepare rice noodles by separating them with gentle massages between your hand and a cutting board.
  6. Mix Chow Fun Sauce.
  7. Heat up the wok to the highest heat for about 1.5 mins. before adding 3 tbsp of cooking oil. Heat until it simmers and add the beef. Cook for 40-60 seconds on high heat, then remove from wok and set aside.
  8. Stir-fry yellow onions and white part of green onion until fragrant, then add the rice noodles, gently stirring until golden brown. Then add the chow fun sauce. Add green parts of scallion and bean sprouts and cook for 30-45 seconds.
  9. Add beef back and cook for a minute before finishing it with 1 tbsp of sesame oil. Taste to adjust the flavors and plate it once it's to your taste!
